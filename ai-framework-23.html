AI PATHWAYS FOR ARTISTS
An open framework for protection, care, and experimentation
Artist-led guidance for AI choices

A guided map to help artists use AI, refuse it, or find the balance in between

This framework turns complex questions about copyright, privacy, ethics, and creativity into a guided map with concrete actions and resources you can use right now. It is part of Digital Ancestors: Mortality, Memory, Machine, a collaboration between artists and responsible AI practitioners exploring how creative work can coexist with emerging technologies.

Backstory

Why we built this framework

Generative AI is reshaping how art is made, circulated, and valued. It can bring up grief, frustration, curiosity, and possibility all at the same time. This project brings together practicing artists, AI researchers, technologists, and policy advisors who care about artist autonomy and responsible technology.

This map grew out of months of creative research and development experiments in Digital Ancestors: Mortality, Memory, Machine. It started with a question: Can AI help care for creative legacy, and cultural memory? We designed gallery works, kept process journals, and tested a number of tools over six months. Alongside our art exhibit, we turned what we learned into an open framework that other artists can adapt, critique, and help build upon.

The map helps clarify what you might want from your own engagement with AI, from opting out entirely to collaborating on new work.
Three pathways

Tracks to match your preferences

You can follow one track or move between them. The map starts where you are and adapts as your needs change.

Protect

Track 1 - Reduce how much work is used for AI training

For artists who want to minimise how much of their work ends up in training datasets or is copied by AI models.

  • Image cloaking tools like Glaze or Nightshade
  • Attribution and watermarking practices
  • Website-level preferences and opt-outs
  • Contract language for client and platform agreements
Admin Support

Track 2 - Use AI for studio admin without oversharing

For artists who want to use AI as a studio assistant. For example, help with writing and planning while keeping control over their data.

  • Prompt tips to improve privacy
  • Automation tools and examples
  • Mainstream and local chat tool options
Co-create

Track 3 - Make new work with AI

For artists experimenting with AI media who want to stay grounded in ethics and transparency.

  • Model choices and data sourcing basics
  • Train models with your own data
  • What current copyright law covers and what it does not

Who it supports

Arts communities and the people around them

The framework is designed for working artists and creative practitioners. It is also useful for cultural workers, arts organizations, educators, policy-makers, and AI builders who want to better understand artist needs.

For artists and creative professionals
Clarify your boundaries, learn practical safety steps, and explore new workflows if you want them. Use the map when posting work, signing agreements, applying for funding, or testing new tools.

For arts and legal organisations
Use the framework in workshops, office hours, or resource hubs. The map can support legal clinics, digital safety training, and collective discussions.
For policy makers
See where artists encounter friction in current tools and contracts. Use the map to surface real-world scenarios when shaping policy.

For AI researchers and developers
Treat the map as a set of user stories that highlight artist concerns around consent, provenance, and creative control.
